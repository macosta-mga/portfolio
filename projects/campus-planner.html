Web App

Recipe and Inventory Tracker

Context

This project creates an inventory and recipe tracker for a local coffee shop to streamline and organize their internal system.

Role

Frontend Design, Web Development

Duration

Janurary 2025 to May 2025

Team

Nirali Shah, Garima Upadhyay, Anirra Kutty, Brittney Chan, Caden Lewis, Divij Muthu, Juyeon Ma, Priya Venkatesan, Zoey Yang

Tools

Figma, HTML, CSS, JavaScript

Background

1951 Coffee Company

1951 Coffee Company is a nonprofit cafe in Berkeley that supports refugees by providing free barista training, work experience, and job placement opportunities. Their mission is to help people rebuild stable lives through employment while also creating a community space that raises awareness about refugee experiences.

Project Overview

A cleaner way to track recipes, supplies, and inventory needs.

Built an inventory and recipe tracking system that manages ingredient quantities, links them to recipes, and alerts the team when restocking is needed, with Amazon data integration and a MongoDB backend. I focused on the frontend, creating interfaces to add and edit recipes and ingredients.

Notification and Backend

Alerting, Data Scraping, Database

  • Data Scraping Team: Collected ingredient data from Amazon (like names, prices, and availability) and structured it so it could be used in the system for tracking and restocking decisions.
  • Database Team (MongoDB): Built and managed the backend database, organizing recipes and ingredients, linking them together, and ensuring all data (quantities, updates, new items) was stored and retrieved efficiently.
  • Alerting Team: Developed the notification system that automatically alerts workers when ingredients are low or need restocking, helping the team stay on top of inventory in real time.

Final Product

Bringing Prototype to Life

I designed and built the recipe and ingredient pages, creating interactive interfaces that allow users to add, edit, and manage recipes and ingredients. I used JavaScript to handle dynamic updates and user interactions, and CSS to create a clean, intuitive, and user-friendly layout.
