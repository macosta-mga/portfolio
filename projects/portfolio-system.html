User Research

Research Project

Context

This project explores how Gen Z college students ages 18-24 think about healthcare and digital booking tools for ZocDoc.

Role

User research, synthesis, interview planning, product insights

Duration

September 2025 to December 2025

Team

Sejal Bilwar, Caroline Tong, Flora Wang, Daniel Huang, Michelle Wu

Tools

Figma, research planning, synthesis, product strategy

Project context

Company Background

Zocdoc is a leading digital healthcare marketplace that helps patients find in-network providers, compare options, and book appointments online.

Their mission is to make healthcare easier by increasing access, transparency, and trust for patients.

Project Scope

Zocdoc partnered with Software Product @ Cal to understand how Gen Z college students (ages 18-24) think about healthcare and digital booking tools.

Why College Students?

Entering Adulthood

College students are navigating healthcare independently for the first time and lack experience with insurance, costs, and selecting providers.

Wide Range of Independence

Students vary dramatically, some rely heavily on parents, while others fully manage their own care.

Barriers to Access

Insurance confusion, cost uncertainty, and limited system knowledge create friction in booking and managing care.

Digital Expectations

Gen Z evaluates service experiences through the lens of the apps they use daily, shaping expectations for healthcare tools.

Our Approach

User interviews to explore students' attitudes, behaviors, barriers, and trust signals.

Qualitative Discovery

Insight Synthesis

Clustered findings and distilled early themes to guide the next phase.

Survey design informed by qualitative insights to measure patterns at scale.

Quantitative Validation

Frameworks & Opportunities

Developed archetypes, journey maps, and key opportunity areas for Zocdoc.

Qualitative discovery

User Interviews

We spoke with 10 college students across majors, years, and independence levels.

Focus Areas:

  • General mindset toward healthcare
  • Gen Z trust and digital mental models
  • Key decision factors (table stakes, comfort signals)
  • Competence indicators
  • Access needs

Insight synthesis

Affinity Mapping

We sorted out all the responses from interviews into relevant categories such as social media reach and importance of transparency when it comes to what truly influences people in their healthcare decisions and what plays a big role in college students' decision making.

We created three user archetypes to represent different student cases.

Journey Map

We created a journey map for each user archetype to represent each patient's journey, mostly depicting their level of independence when it comes to their own healthcare.

Initial Takeaways

01

Social media shapes expectations

Students learn from TikTok, but rarely choose providers from it.

02

Independence varies widely

Some students fully self-manage care; others rely on parents for every step.

03

Clear communication builds Trust

Straightforward messaging reassures students and builds confidence.

We then synthesized and quantified the research we conducted, summarizing three key insights. From there, we developed three recommendations for each insight to validate our findings and strengthen Zocdoc's approach to bridging Gen Z's expectations for healthcare apps with the Zocdoc platform. This information is confidential to our clients!
